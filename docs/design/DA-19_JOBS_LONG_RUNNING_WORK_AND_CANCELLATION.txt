===============================================================================
DESIGN AREA DA-19 - JOBS, LONG-RUNNING WORK & CANCELLATION
===============================================================================

Status:
-------
EVOLVING

Description:
------------
Defines Job identity, Job lifecycle boundaries, cancellation semantics, and
Job-owned trace reason identifiers for asynchronous or long-running work.

This document is intentionally minimal at creation time. It exists to give
Job lifecycle semantics a proper ownership home instead of leaving Jobs as
memory-only design discussion or placing Job-specific lifecycle rules in the
tracing substrate.

Purpose:
--------
This document defines the Turnix Job lifecycle and identity anchor.

It defines:
- Job as explicit requested work with lifecycle state
- Job identity and owner correlation
- relationship between Jobs and Orchestration Units
- Job completion, failure, cancellation, timeout, and safe-point pause
  boundaries
- Job-domain trace reason identifiers
- minimum trace evidence for Job lifecycle events

It does NOT define:
- trace event structure, trace DAG semantics, or span mechanics (see DA-03)
- authoritative committed state or transaction semantics (see DA-04)
- Window, SafePointWindow, or AbortedWindow semantics (see DA-08)
- pack activation semantics or PackContext authority (see DA-14)
- service, capability, or permission semantics (see DA-16)
- debugger UI layout or breakpoint behavior (see DA-18)
- concrete RPC message schemas or transport behavior (see IS-01 and IS-02)

===============================================================================
01. INTERPRETATION BASIS
===============================================================================

01.1. This document expresses normative meaning through explicit,
      deterministic prose.

01.2. Normative meaning in Turnix derives from sentence structure and
      document context rather than from RFC-style keywords.

01.3. The CORE META-RULES documents define the general rule language and
      interpretation model used by this document.

01.4. This document defines Job lifecycle, identity, and cancellation
      semantics within that shared interpretation model.


===============================================================================
02. CORE CONCEPT
===============================================================================

02.1. A Job answers:

          "What requested work is waiting, running, completed, failed,
           cancelled, timed out, or paused at a safe point?"

02.2. A Job is an explicit lifecycle object representing requested work.

02.3. A Job may represent short work or long-running work.

02.4. A Job has identity independent of any one RPC message, UI request,
      or transport event.

02.5. A Job provides a stable handle for:
      - completion signaling
      - progress reporting
      - failure reporting
      - cancellation request
      - timeout handling
      - safe-point pause interaction when supported

02.6. A Job is not merely a Promise, coroutine, callback, thread, or task.

02.7. A Job may be implemented using runtime primitives, but those runtime
      primitives do not define the Job lifecycle semantics.

02.8. A Job does not become authoritative committed state merely because it
      exists, runs, or completes.

02.9. Job effects become authoritative only through the state, transaction,
      Window, capability, and persistence rules owned by the documents that
      govern those effects.


===============================================================================
03. JOB IDENTITY
===============================================================================

03.1. A jobId identifies one Job.

03.2. jobId is stable for the lifetime of that Job.

03.3. jobId is not reused for a different Job during the same appInstance
      active region.

03.4. jobId is the correlation identity used by Job-owned trace evidence.

03.5. jobId does not identify the result payload by itself.

03.6. jobId does not identify an Orchestration Unit by itself.

03.7. jobId does not identify an RPC request by itself.


===============================================================================
04. JOB OWNERSHIP AND CORRELATION
===============================================================================

04.1. A Job has an owning scope.

04.2. The owning scope may include:
      - appInstanceId
      - orchestrationUnitId
      - caller pack identity
      - capability identifier
      - RPC request identity
      - debugger request identity
      - user decision identity

04.3. Identifier presence varies by Job origin.

04.4. Missing optional correlation identifiers do not invalidate the Job.

04.5. Required correlation identifiers for a concrete Job class are defined
      by the document or implementation specification that creates that Job
      class.

04.6. This document defines the shared Job identity and lifecycle boundary.

04.7. This document does not define every possible Job origin.


===============================================================================
05. RELATION TO ORCHESTRATION UNITS
===============================================================================

05.1. A Job may be backed by one Orchestration Unit.

05.2. A Job may coordinate multiple Orchestration Units when a later
      document explicitly defines that behavior.

05.3. An Orchestration Unit may be associated with a Job when the unit is
      performing requested work that requires Job lifecycle visibility.

05.4. Not every Orchestration Unit is necessarily a Job.

05.5. Not every Job detail is necessarily represented by an Orchestration
      Unit.

05.6. Job lifecycle identity and Orchestration Unit mutation authority remain
      distinct concepts.

05.7. State mutation authority remains constrained by DA-04 even when work
      is represented as a Job.


===============================================================================
06. JOB LIFECYCLE
===============================================================================

06.1. A Job lifecycle has explicit observable outcomes.

06.2. A Job may be:
      - created
      - queued
      - running
      - completed
      - failed
      - cancellation requested
      - cancelled
      - timed out
      - pause requested
      - paused at a safe point
      - resumed

06.3. A Job reaches at most one terminal outcome.

06.4. Terminal outcomes include:
      - completed
      - failed
      - cancelled
      - timed out

06.5. A terminal Job does not later become running again.

06.6. Retry is modeled as new work unless a later document explicitly
      defines a retry relationship.

06.7. A retry does not reuse the previous jobId.

06.8. A Job may report progress while it is non-terminal.

06.9. Progress reporting does not by itself imply committed state mutation.

06.10. Completion reports the Job's requested work outcome. It does not by
       itself define whether any side effect became authoritative.


===============================================================================
07. CANCELLATION, TIMEOUT, AND DISCARDING WORK
===============================================================================

07.1. Cancellation is explicit.

07.2. Cancellation request and cancellation outcome are distinct lifecycle
      events.

07.3. A cancellation request does not guarantee immediate cancellation.

07.4. A Job may refuse or defer cancellation only through explicit policy or
      explicit lifecycle semantics defined by the Job owner.

07.5. Cancelling a Job discards the Job's uncommitted work from the
      perspective of the Job result.

07.6. Cancellation does not silently roll back committed state.

07.7. Committed state produced before cancellation remains governed by the
      state and Window documents that own committed-state meaning.

07.8. Timeout is an explicit lifecycle outcome or failure cause.

07.9. Timeout does not silently convert into cancellation unless the owning
      Job semantics define that conversion explicitly.

07.10. Cancellation and timeout remain trace-visible.


===============================================================================
08. SAFE-POINT PAUSE AND DEBUGGER INTERACTION
===============================================================================

08.1. A Job may support pause-at-safe-point behavior.

08.2. Pause-at-safe-point is explicit.

08.3. Pause request and pause achievement are distinct lifecycle events.

08.4. A Job that cannot reach a safe point reports that limitation
      explicitly.

08.5. Safe-point pause does not silently cancel the Job.

08.6. Debugger breakpoint and inspection behavior remains constrained by
      DA-18.

08.7. Window creation waiting and safe-point policy remain constrained by
      DA-08.


===============================================================================
09. JOB TRACE REASON IDENTIFIERS
===============================================================================

09.1. DA-19 owns the Job-domain trace reason identifiers used for Job
      lifecycle, progress, cancellation, timeout, and safe-point pause
      events.

09.2. The following Job-domain trace reasons are defined:

      - JobCreated
      - JobQueued
      - JobStarted
      - JobProgressReported
      - JobCompleted
      - JobFailed
      - JobCancellationRequested
      - JobCancelled
      - JobTimeoutReached
      - JobPauseAtSafePointRequested
      - JobPausedAtSafePoint
      - JobPauseDeferred
      - JobResumed

09.3. JobCreated is emitted when a Job object is created.

09.4. JobQueued is emitted when a Job is accepted but not yet running.

09.5. JobStarted is emitted when Job execution begins.

09.6. JobProgressReported is emitted when a Job reports progress.

09.7. JobCompleted is emitted when a Job completes successfully.

09.8. JobFailed is emitted when a Job ends in failure.

09.9. JobCancellationRequested is emitted when cancellation is requested for
      a Job.

09.10. JobCancelled is emitted when a Job reaches the cancelled terminal
       outcome.

09.11. JobTimeoutReached is emitted when a Job reaches its timeout boundary.

09.12. JobPauseAtSafePointRequested is emitted when pause-at-safe-point is
       requested for a Job.

09.13. JobPausedAtSafePoint is emitted when a Job reaches and applies a
       safe-point pause.

09.14. JobPauseDeferred is emitted when a Job cannot pause immediately but
       the pause request remains pending or deferred by policy.

09.15. JobResumed is emitted when a paused Job resumes.

09.16. Job lifecycle trace evidence records enough context to explain:
       - jobId
       - appInstanceId when applicable
       - orchestrationUnitId when applicable
       - caller pack identity when applicable
       - capability identifier when applicable
       - RPC request identity when applicable
       - debugger request identity when applicable
       - terminal outcome when the Job reaches one
       - failureReasonCategory when the Job fails
       - cancelReasonCategory when cancellation completes
       - timeoutPolicyId when timeout policy participates
       - safePointDescriptor when a safe point is reached

09.17. DA-03 defines the trace substrate used to represent Job lifecycle
       trace evidence.

09.18. DA-19 defines only the Job-domain ownership of these trace reasons
       and required correlation evidence.


===============================================================================
10. EXPLICITLY OPEN QUESTIONS
===============================================================================

10.1. OPEN questions include:
      - exact Job state machine shape
      - whether all Jobs require queued state
      - whether timeout is terminal outcome or failure subclass in concrete
        APIs
      - exact progress payload semantics
      - exact relationship between Jobs, RPC requests, subscriptions, and
        capability calls
      - exact Job result envelope structure
      - exact cancellation refusal or deferral taxonomy

10.2. These questions remain open because this document currently exists to
      establish ownership boundaries needed by the DA-03 tracing migration
      and the upcoming Job proposal integration.


===============================================================================
11. LOCKED SUMMARY
===============================================================================

11.1. LOCKED:
      - Job is an explicit lifecycle object representing requested work
      - Job identity is distinct from RPC request identity
      - Job identity is distinct from Orchestration Unit identity
      - jobId is the Job correlation identity
      - Job lifecycle has observable terminal outcomes
      - cancellation request and cancellation outcome are distinct
      - timeout is explicit
      - safe-point pause is explicit
      - Job-domain trace reason identifiers are owned by DA-19
      - DA-03 owns the trace substrate, not Job lifecycle semantics

11.2. OPEN:
      - detailed Job lifecycle workflows beyond identity, cancellation,
        timeout, safe-point, and trace-ownership boundaries
