===============================================================================
DESIGN AREA DA-29 - PACK MANAGEMENT, CATALOG OPERATIONS AND GRAPH PREPARATION
===============================================================================

Status:
-------
EVOLVING

Description:
------------
Defines PackManager as the official Turnix pack-management and graph-
preparation domain, defines pack catalog operation semantics, separates
PackManager tooling from discovery, resolution, importing, activation,
capability authority, and mechanical loading, and defines how advisory pack
relations, hard dependencies, user policy, SavePack history, and selected graph
intent are handled before activation.

This document owns PackManager semantic boundaries. It consumes root discovery,
pack identity, generic resolution, importing, dependency, activation, service,
capability, permission, policy, RuntimeHost, AppInstance, SavePack, and adapter
semantics defined by earlier design areas.

Purpose:
--------
This document defines how Turnix inspects, presents, prepares, modifies, and
explains pack catalogs and selected pack graphs without allowing helpful tooling
to become hidden loader, resolver, activation, import, persistence, or
capability authority.

It defines:
- PackManager as an official Turnix management and graph-preparation domain
- pack catalog operation meaning
- ListPacks as the general listing primitive
- AppPack, SavePack, ViewPack, ContentPack, and ModPack listing as filters over
  Pack kind
- PackManager scope across RuntimeHost, workspace, AppPack, SavePack,
  AppInstance, and tooling contexts
- PackManager relationship to roots, discovery, visibility, generic resolution,
  dependencies, advisory pack relations, imports, activation, capabilities,
  decisions, persistence, RuntimeHost, AppInstance, Views, and adapters
- selected pack graph and activation-plan preparation meaning
- advisory conflict, recommendation, and compatibility presentation semantics
- hard dependency handling boundary
- user and policy override boundary
- expected-break semantics for explicitly accepted risky graphs
- PackManager view and command/capability access boundaries
- PackManager trace reason ownership
- explicitly unsupported PackManager bypass behavior

It does NOT define:
- root discovery boundaries or filesystem scanning rules (see DA-09)
- pack identity, hierarchy, visibility, or SavePack visibility semantics
  (see DA-10)
- generic PackRequest resolution mechanics (see DA-11)
- importing, module loading, or code-boundary rules (see DA-12)
- hard dependency, provider-role, pinned, copied, or advisory-relation field
  semantics (see DA-13)
- activation lifecycle or PackContext authority (see DA-14)
- visibility/export access-control mechanics beyond DA-10 and DA-15
- service, capability, permission, or Job-backed capability semantics
  (see DA-16)
- user decision and remembered policy semantics (see DA-17)
- Job lifecycle, timeout, cancellation, progress, or result semantics
  (see DA-19)
- AppInstance lifecycle or runtime identity semantics (see DA-20)
- RPC, channel, or transport semantics (see DA-21)
- UI, View, or frontend runtime semantics (see DA-22)
- execution adapter boundary or adapter lifecycle semantics (see DA-23 and
  DA-27)
- RuntimeHost lifecycle, startup mode, command surface, or workspace ownership
  semantics (see DA-28)
- concrete pack catalog result envelopes
- concrete resolved graph schema
- concrete activation-plan schema
- concrete PackManager UI layout
- concrete internet search, registry, package-index, install, update, or delete
  protocols
- concrete command syntax or RPC envelope fields

===============================================================================
01. INTERPRETATION BASIS
===============================================================================

01.1. This document expresses normative meaning through explicit,
      deterministic prose.

01.2. Normative meaning in Turnix derives from sentence structure and document
      context rather than from RFC-style keywords.

01.3. The CORE META-RULES documents define the general rule language and
      interpretation model used by this document.

01.4. This document defines PackManager, pack catalog, and graph-preparation
      semantics within that shared interpretation model.


===============================================================================
02. CORE CONCEPT
===============================================================================

02.1. Pack management answers:

          "How does Turnix help users and tools inspect, assemble, repair,
           update, and explain pack graphs without hiding resolution,
           activation, import, capability, policy, persistence, or loader
           authority behind convenience?"

02.2. PackManager is the official Turnix pack-management and graph-preparation
      domain.

02.3. PackManager prepares, explains, validates, repairs, and manages selected
      pack graph intent.

02.4. PackManager is helpful tooling and management authority surface.

02.5. PackManager is not Kernel authority by itself.

02.6. PackManager is not discovery.

02.7. PackManager is not generic resolution.

02.8. PackManager is not importing.

02.9. PackManager is not activation.

02.10. PackManager is not an execution adapter.

02.11. PackManager is not a mechanical loader.

02.12. PackManager is not capability permission.

02.13. PackManager is not persistence authority by itself.

02.14. PackManager is not a View by itself.

02.15. PackManager may expose Views, command surfaces, capability surfaces,
       RPC operations, and test surfaces that present or request PackManager
       operations.

02.16. Those surfaces do not become authority merely because they can present
       or request PackManager behavior.

02.17. PackManager helps choose and explain what Turnix will try.

02.18. Turnix still performs only the accepted graph, activation plan, policy,
       and operation requested through Kernel-mediated boundaries.

02.19. Later sections refine and apply these rules. They do not weaken them.


===============================================================================
03. PACKMANAGER IDENTITY AND ROLE
===============================================================================

03.1. PackManager is a Turnix-recognized management domain for pack catalogs,
      pack sources, selected graphs, compatibility evidence, advisory
      relations, and graph-preparation workflows.

03.2. PackManager may be implemented by first-party Turnix tooling.

03.3. PackManager may have multiple entry surfaces.

03.4. PackManager entry surfaces may include:
      - RuntimeHost command surface
      - Launcher View
      - dedicated PackManager View
      - AI assistant or buddy View
      - developer tool View
      - application-embedded View
      - CLI surface
      - LAN or remote client surface
      - test surface

03.5. A PackManager View is a presentation and interaction surface.

03.6. A PackManager View is not PackManager authority by itself.

03.7. A pack or View displaying PackManager UI does not own PackManager
      authority merely because it displays that UI.

03.8. A game, application, launcher, or tool may request PackManager behavior
      only through Turnix-mediated command, capability, RPC, or equivalent
      Kernel-owned surfaces.

03.9. PackManager operations may exist before any AppInstance exists.

03.10. PackManager operations may also be requested from an AppInstance when
       policy, capability, RuntimeHost, and authority boundaries permit that
       request.

03.11. PackManager is official Turnix tooling, not third-party hidden runtime
       behavior.

03.12. Official tooling status does not permit PackManager to bypass Kernel,
       persistence, policy, capability, resolution, activation, adapter, or
       trace boundaries.


===============================================================================
04. PACK CATALOG OPERATIONS
===============================================================================

04.1. A pack catalog is the Turnix-visible population of discovered and indexed
      pack descriptors available to a catalog operation under an explicit
      catalog context.

04.2. The pack catalog is derived from discovery and indexing.

04.3. The pack catalog does not perform discovery by being read.

04.4. Catalog operations do not lazily rescan the filesystem unless a separate
      explicit rescan operation is requested and accepted.

04.5. ListPacks is the general pack-listing primitive.

04.6. ListPacks may be filtered by pack kind.

04.7. AppPack listing is ListPacks filtered to AppPack kind.

04.8. SavePack listing is ListPacks filtered to SavePack kind and relevant
      save roots or save contexts.

04.9. ViewPack listing is ListPacks filtered to ViewPack kind.

04.10. ContentPack listing is ListPacks filtered to ContentPack kind.

04.11. ModPack listing is ListPacks filtered to ModPack kind.

04.12. ListPacks may also be filtered by:
       - root or root class
       - workspace
       - active save context
       - visibility context
       - AppPack compatibility context
       - SavePack compatibility context
       - search text
       - author
       - version or version range
       - installation source
       - trust or source classification
       - enabled, disabled, or selected graph state

04.13. A ListPacks result means the listed pack descriptors are visible to that
       catalog operation under the supplied catalog context.

04.14. A listed pack is not necessarily selected for a graph.

04.15. A listed pack is not necessarily a dependency outcome.

04.16. A listed pack is not necessarily activated.

04.17. A listed pack is not necessarily importable.

04.18. A listed pack does not grant capability permission.

04.19. Catalog presentation preserves the distinction between existence,
       visibility, resolution eligibility, dependency satisfaction, selected
       graph membership, activation state, and capability availability.


===============================================================================
05. PACKMANAGER SCOPES
===============================================================================

05.1. PackManager operations occur inside an explicit management scope.

05.2. PackManager scopes may include:
      - RuntimeHost scope
      - workspace scope
      - AppPack scope
      - SavePack scope
      - AppInstance scope
      - tooling scope
      - test scope

05.3. RuntimeHost scope concerns pack catalogs, roots, installed packs,
      package sources, and selected graph operations available to the running
      RuntimeHost.

05.4. Workspace scope concerns pack catalogs, configuration, source state,
      and installed or available pack material associated with one workspace.

05.5. AppPack scope concerns graph preparation, dependency checks,
      advisory-relation presentation, and compatibility for one AppPack.

05.6. SavePack scope concerns copied packs, pinned packs, last-known-good
      graph evidence, save-local material, compatibility checks, repair,
      export, and import for one SavePack.

05.7. AppInstance scope concerns active graph inspection, active activation
      evidence, pending graph changes, and operations that may affect a running
      AppInstance.

05.8. Tooling scope concerns development, audit, diagnostics, authoring,
      package-index work, test behavior, or maintenance behavior not directly
      owned by one AppInstance.

05.9. Test scope does not weaken runtime authority boundaries.

05.10. Scope does not grant authority by itself.

05.11. Each operation remains constrained by the owning authority documents for
       the resources it touches.


===============================================================================
06. RELATION TO ROOTS AND DISCOVERY
===============================================================================

06.1. Root and discovery semantics are defined by DA-09.

06.2. PackManager consumes discovered and indexed pack descriptors.

06.3. PackManager does not redefine root classes.

06.4. PackManager does not redefine content-root, userdata-root, or saves-root
      meaning.

06.5. PackManager may request explicit rescan, install, update, remove,
      repair, or source-refresh operations when those operations are defined
      and authorized.

06.6. Those operations remain subject to root, persistence, policy, and
      authority boundaries.

06.7. PackManager does not silently write into first-party, third-party,
      custom, userdata, or saves roots.

06.8. PackManager may be the authorized tool surface that performs pack
      installation, update, deletion, or replacement when the relevant root and
      policy rules permit that operation.

06.9. PackManager installation authority does not make runtime pack code a root
      writer.

06.10. PackManager root modifications are trace-visible.


===============================================================================
07. RELATION TO PACK IDENTITY AND VISIBILITY
===============================================================================

07.1. Pack identity, hierarchy, pack kind, and visibility semantics are defined
      by DA-10.

07.2. PackManager does not redefine pack identity.

07.3. PackManager does not redefine pack kind.

07.4. PackManager does not redefine visibility.

07.5. PackManager may present visibility graphs, hidden packs, shadowed packs,
      save-local packs, and visibility failures when the operation and policy
      permit that presentation.

07.6. Visibility presentation does not grant visibility.

07.7. Visibility does not grant capability authority.

07.8. PackManager does not use visibility as capability authority.

07.9. PackManager may explain that a pack exists but is not visible from a
      requested context.

07.10. PackManager may help users or tooling choose a graph that respects
       visibility constraints.

07.11. PackManager does not silently bypass visibility constraints to make a
       graph work.


===============================================================================
08. RELATION TO GENERIC RESOLUTION
===============================================================================

08.1. Generic resolution semantics are defined by DA-11.

08.2. PackManager consumes generic resolution outcomes.

08.3. PackManager may call generic resolution with an explicit resolution
      context.

08.4. PackManager does not redefine PackRequest grammar.

08.5. PackManager does not merge manifests.

08.6. PackManager does not turn resolution into discovery.

08.7. PackManager does not silently switch resolution contexts.

08.8. PackManager may present resolution candidates, hard rejections, soft
      constraints, ambiguities, selected candidates, and resolution failures.

08.9. PackManager may ask user or policy to resolve ambiguity when the owning
      resolution consumer permits that path.

08.10. PackManager may persist accepted ambiguity decisions only through the
       surrounding policy or consumer authority that owns such persistence.

08.11. PackManager does not persist resolution decisions by hidden side effect.


===============================================================================
09. RELATION TO DEPENDENCIES AND ADVISORY PACK RELATIONS
===============================================================================

09.1. Hard dependency and advisory pack-relation semantics are defined by
      DA-13.

09.2. PackManager consumes hard dependency declarations.

09.3. PackManager consumes provider-role metadata.

09.4. PackManager consumes advisory pack relations.

09.5. PackManager may present:
      - missing hard dependencies
      - unsatisfied version requirements
      - copied SavePack dependencies
      - pinned SavePack dependencies
      - provider-role availability
      - supportedPacks compatibility evidence
      - conflictingPacks warnings
      - recommendedPacks suggestions

09.6. PackManager does not convert advisory pack relations into hard
      dependencies.

09.7. PackManager does not convert advisory pack relations into hard
      activation blockers by itself.

09.8. PackManager does not convert advisory pack relations into loader rules by
      itself.

09.9. PackManager may let policy or user decision turn an advisory warning into
      a graph-selection decision for a specific selected graph.

09.10. Such a graph-selection decision is explicit, scoped, trace-visible, and
       does not rewrite the manifest advisory relation.

09.11. Hard dependency failure remains hard failure unless an accepted policy or
       user decision explicitly resolves it under DA-13 and DA-17.

09.12. Advisory conflict override is not the same as hard dependency
       satisfaction.

09.13. PackManager may explain both categories, but it does not collapse them.


===============================================================================
10. SELECTED PACK GRAPH AND ACTIVATION PLAN
===============================================================================

10.1. A selected pack graph is the explicit graph intent that Turnix will use
      for later runtime or tooling operations.

10.2. A selected pack graph may include:
       - graph identity when defined
       - graph purpose
       - AppPack identity when applicable
       - SavePack identity when applicable
       - selected pack identities
       - selected versions
       - selected source locations or content identities when applicable
       - dependency edges
       - provider-role choices when applicable
       - advisory warnings
       - accepted overrides
       - policy decisions
       - user decisions
       - compatibility evidence
       - save-local copied pack references
       - pinned pack references
       - origin or provenance evidence when available

10.3. An activation plan is selected graph intent refined for activation order
      and activation readiness.

10.4. Activation lifecycle semantics are defined by DA-14.

10.5. PackManager may prepare an activation plan.

10.6. PackManager does not activate packs by preparing an activation plan.

10.7. PackManager does not execute pack code by preparing a selected pack
      graph.

10.8. PackManager does not import code by preparing a selected pack graph.

10.9. PackManager does not grant PackContext authority by preparing a selected
      pack graph.

10.10. Activation consumes the accepted activation plan through DA-14 and
       related execution-boundary documents.

10.11. An accepted graph or activation plan may still fail during activation.

10.12. Such failure is activation evidence, not proof that PackManager owned
       activation.

10.13. PackManager may contribute diagnostic context to explain activation
       failure where graph evidence is relevant.


===============================================================================
11. EXPECTED-BREAK AND RISKY GRAPH SEMANTICS
===============================================================================

11.1. PackManager may detect graphs with warnings, advisory conflicts,
      unsupported combinations, missing optional material, soft constraints, or
      other risk evidence.

11.2. PackManager may present those risks to the user or policy surface.

11.3. User or policy may explicitly accept a risky graph when the governing
      documents permit that choice.

11.4. An accepted risky graph remains trace-visible.

11.5. Risk acceptance does not make the graph safe.

11.6. Risk acceptance does not rewrite pack metadata.

11.7. Risk acceptance does not satisfy hard dependencies unless the accepted
      decision explicitly resolves the hard dependency failure through an
      allowed path.

11.8. Risk acceptance does not grant capability permission.

11.9. Risk acceptance does not grant execution authorization for unsafe code.

11.10. If an accepted risky graph later fails, the failure is expected
       evidence in the sense that the risk was known and recorded.

11.11. Expected-break evidence does not convert failure into success.

11.12. Expected-break evidence helps diagnostics explain that the run used an
       explicitly accepted risky graph.

11.13. PackManager does not hide failure merely because risk was accepted.


===============================================================================
12. RELATION TO IMPORTING AND MODULE LOADING
===============================================================================

12.1. Importing and module-loading semantics are defined by DA-12.

12.2. PackManager does not import pack code as part of catalog listing.

12.3. PackManager does not import pack code as part of graph preparation.

12.4. PackManager does not use native imports to inspect pack runtime symbols.

12.5. PackManager does not bypass turnixImport, dependency consistency,
      visibility, activation, or execution authority.

12.6. PackManager may inspect metadata, manifests, indexes, graph evidence,
      and trace evidence through authorized non-executing paths.

12.7. Metadata inspection does not become code execution.

12.8. Code import or adapter artifact loading remains governed by DA-12,
      DA-14, DA-23, and DA-27.


===============================================================================
13. RELATION TO ACTIVATION
===============================================================================

13.1. Activation semantics are defined by DA-14.

13.2. PackManager may influence activation order by preparing the accepted
      graph or activation plan.

13.3. PackManager does not activate packs by itself.

13.4. PackManager does not call onLoad(ctx) by itself.

13.5. PackManager does not create PackContext authority by itself.

13.6. PackManager may present activation readiness, activation ordering,
      dependency blocking, and activation failure diagnostics.

13.7. Activation failure remains activation-domain evidence under DA-14.

13.8. PackManager diagnostics do not replace activation trace evidence.

13.9. PackManager warnings do not become activation outcomes.

13.10. An activation executor does not perform PackManager compatibility
       planning by itself.

13.11. Mechanical loading, adapter artifact loading, importing, and activation
       execution do not perform PackManager policy or graph-preparation work by
       themselves.


===============================================================================
14. RELATION TO SERVICES, CAPABILITIES AND PERMISSIONS
===============================================================================

14.1. Service, capability, and permission semantics are defined by DA-16.

14.2. PackManager operations may be exposed through RuntimeHost commands.

14.3. PackManager operations may be exposed through capabilities when pack,
      View, adapter-executed code, or cross-pack runtime behavior requests
      PackManager behavior.

14.4. PackManager operations may be exposed through RPC or equivalent
      mediated request surfaces.

14.5. PackManager operations that install, update, remove, download, search
      external sources, modify selected graphs, copy packs into SavePacks,
      export SavePacks, or change persisted state require appropriate
      permission, policy, persistence, and trace handling.

14.6. A PackManager service, if one exists internally, is runtime wiring only.

14.7. A PackManager service is not a cross-pack authority bypass.

14.8. AppPacks, ViewPacks, tools, tests, AI buddy surfaces, games, or launcher
      surfaces do not receive raw PackManager authority merely because they can
      display PackManager UI.

14.9. Capability-mediated PackManager operations remain capability-mediated.

14.10. RuntimeHost-command PackManager operations remain RuntimeHost-command
       operations and do not become pack authority.

14.11. PackManager does not grant capability permission.

14.12. PackManager does not resolve capability export conflicts silently.

14.13. PackManager may help present capability conflicts, but DA-16 governs the
       conflict outcome.


===============================================================================
15. RELATION TO POLICY AND USER DECISIONS
===============================================================================

15.1. User decision and remembered policy semantics are defined by DA-17.

15.2. PackManager may request decisions for graph ambiguity, advisory risk,
      source trust, unsafe operation, install/update/delete confirmation,
      hard dependency resolution where allowed, or SavePack graph repair.

15.3. PackManager does not own remembered-decision precedence.

15.4. PackManager does not silently remember user decisions.

15.5. PackManager does not silently broaden remembered decisions to new packs,
      new versions, new roots, new sources, new SavePacks, new AppInstances, or
      new trust categories.

15.6. PackManager decision prompts preserve the distinction between:
      - advisory warning and hard dependency failure
      - risk acceptance and safety
      - source trust and capability permission
      - graph selection and activation success
      - install/update permission and runtime execution permission
      - for-this-run and remembered decisions

15.7. PackManager decisions remain trace-visible.


===============================================================================
16. RELATION TO PERSISTENCE, CONFIGURATION AND STATE
===============================================================================

16.1. Authoritative state and transaction semantics are defined by DA-04.

16.2. Configuration and schema semantics are defined by DA-06.

16.3. Persistent I/O authority is defined by DA-07.

16.4. PackManager operations may read or write:
      - catalog caches
      - package source configuration
      - installed pack records
      - selected graph records
      - SavePack graph evidence
      - copied pack material where permitted
      - pinned pack references where permitted
      - user decisions through DA-17-owned mechanisms
      - tool state through the owning state/configuration mechanism

16.5. PackManager writes occur only through Kernel-mediated persistence,
      configuration, transaction, and policy boundaries.

16.6. PackManager does not write into pack content roots unless the operation
      is an authorized pack-management operation for that root.

16.7. PackManager does not make runtime pack code a writer for installed pack
      roots.

16.8. PackManager does not mutate SavePack state except through SavePack,
      persistence, policy, and authority rules.

16.9. PackManager persistence failure is explicit.

16.10. PackManager persistence failure does not silently become successful
       graph preparation.


===============================================================================
17. RELATION TO RUNTIMEHOST, APPINSTANCE AND SAVEPACK
===============================================================================

17.1. RuntimeHost semantics are defined by DA-28.

17.2. AppInstance lifecycle semantics are defined by DA-20.

17.3. SavePack binding and writable ownership semantics are defined by DA-20
      and related save/persistence documents.

17.4. PackManager may operate at RuntimeHost scope before any AppInstance
      exists.

17.5. PackManager may prepare a graph for AppInstance creation.

17.6. PackManager may check last-used or last-known-good graph evidence for a
      SavePack before AppInstance creation.

17.7. PackManager may inspect active graph evidence for an AppInstance when
      policy permits that inspection.

17.8. PackManager may prepare graph changes for future AppInstance load,
      restart, or migration.

17.9. PackManager does not silently mutate active AppInstance graph state.

17.10. PackManager does not silently update packs that are active in a running
       AppInstance.

17.11. PackManager does not silently make changes to a SavePack-owned copied
       pack graph.

17.12. PackManager graph changes affecting a running AppInstance require
       explicit lifecycle, policy, and trace handling.

17.13. SavePack last-known-good fallback remains governed by DA-13 and
       activation behavior.


===============================================================================
18. RELATION TO VIEWS, UI AND FRONTEND RUNTIME
===============================================================================

18.1. UI, View, and frontend runtime semantics are defined by DA-22.

18.2. PackManager may expose one or more Views.

18.3. PackManager Views are presentation and interaction surfaces.

18.4. PackManager Views may be displayed by:
      - Turnix Default Launcher
      - dedicated PackManager tooling
      - AI buddy surfaces
      - application surfaces
      - developer tools
      - test tools

18.5. Displaying a PackManager View does not grant pack-management authority to
      the displaying surface.

18.6. Frontend PackManager code remains adapter-executed frontend code.

18.7. Frontend PackManager code does not directly mutate pack catalogs,
      selected graphs, roots, SavePacks, or configuration.

18.8. Frontend PackManager commands become meaningful only when Kernel-owned
      authority accepts the corresponding command, capability request, RPC
      message, decision, or runtime operation.

18.9. PackManager UI may present advisory warnings, hard failures, risk
      decisions, graph previews, source trust, install/update/delete actions,
      active graph state, and SavePack graph evidence.

18.10. PackManager UI does not own the semantics it presents.


===============================================================================
19. RELATION TO EXECUTION ADAPTERS
===============================================================================

19.1. Execution adapter boundary semantics are defined by DA-23.

19.2. Adapter lifecycle, trust, isolation, and adapter-domain trace semantics
      are defined by DA-27.

19.3. PackManager does not gain adapter authority by selecting or presenting an
      adapter-related graph.

19.4. Adapter selection for executable artifacts remains governed by DA-23 and
      DA-27.

19.5. PackManager may present adapter requirements, adapter availability,
      adapter trust warnings, or adapter incompatibilities.

19.6. PackManager presentation of adapter evidence does not authorize adapter
      execution.

19.7. Adapter execution authorization remains distinct from PackManager graph
      preparation.

19.8. Adapter failure does not become PackManager failure by default.

19.9. PackManager graph problems do not become adapter failure by default.

21.1. PackManager trace evidence records enough context, when available, to

      - PackManager operation identity
      - PackManager scope
      - runtimeHostId when applicable
      - workspace identity when applicable
      - appInstanceId when applicable
      - AppPack identity when applicable
      - SavePack identity when applicable
      - requesting surface identity when applicable
      - requesting pack identity when applicable
      - viewId when applicable
      - RPC message identity when applicable
      - capability identity when applicable
      - jobId when applicable
      - orchestrationUnitId when applicable
      - PackRequest when applicable
      - selected pack identity when applicable
      - candidate pack identity when applicable
      - dependency edge when applicable
      - advisory relation kind when applicable
      - provider role when applicable
      - source identity when applicable
      - root identity when applicable
      - user or policy decision identity when applicable
      - warning or conflict category when applicable
      - failure reason category when failure occurs

21.2. PackManager trace evidence does not replace resolution trace evidence.

21.3. PackManager trace evidence does not replace activation trace evidence.

21.4. PackManager trace evidence does not replace capability trace evidence.

21.5. PackManager trace evidence does not replace RuntimeHost trace evidence.

21.6. PackManager trace evidence does not replace persistence trace evidence.

21.7. PackManager trace evidence preserves causal links to resolution,
      decision, capability, RuntimeHost, Job, AppInstance, SavePack,
      activation, and persistence evidence when those events exist.

       - root meaning
       - pack identity
       - visibility
       - generic resolution
       - dependency semantics
       - advisory relation meaning
       - importing boundaries
       - activation lifecycle
       - service and capability authority
       - user decision and policy semantics
       - persistence authority
       - RuntimeHost and AppInstance lifecycle
       - adapter authority boundaries

22.3. If a PackManager implementation requires a semantic rule not defined
      here, this document is updated first or alongside that implementation
      specification.


===============================================================================
23. WHAT IS EXPLICITLY NOT SUPPORTED
===============================================================================

23.1. The following are not supported:
      - PackManager replacing Kernel authority
      - PackManager replacing discovery
      - PackManager replacing generic resolution
      - PackManager redefining pack identity or visibility
      - PackManager redefining hard dependency semantics
      - PackManager converting advisory relations into hard dependencies by
        itself
      - PackManager converting advisory relations into activation blockers by
        itself
      - PackManager importing pack code during catalog listing or graph
        preparation
      - PackManager activating packs by preparing a graph
      - PackManager calling onLoad(ctx) by itself
      - PackManager creating PackContext authority by itself
      - PackManager granting capability permission
      - PackManager bypassing capability mediation for cross-pack operations
      - PackManager exposing raw management authority through a View
      - PackManager service objects being used as cross-pack authority bypass
      - PackManager silently writing roots, configuration, SavePacks, selected
        graphs, or policy decisions
      - PackManager silently resolving ambiguity
      - PackManager silently accepting risky graphs
      - PackManager silently broadening remembered decisions
      - PackManager silently updating active packs used by a running
        AppInstance
      - PackManager warnings being treated as activation outcomes
      - mechanical loaders, importers, adapter artifact loaders, or activation
        executors performing PackManager policy or graph-preparation work by
        themselves

===============================================================================
25. LOCKED SUMMARY
===============================================================================

25.1. LOCKED:
      - PackManager is the official Turnix pack-management and graph-
        preparation domain.
      - PackManager prepares, explains, validates, repairs, and manages
        selected pack graph intent.
      - PackManager is not Kernel authority by itself.
      - PackManager is not discovery, generic resolution, importing,
        activation, adapter execution, mechanical loading, capability
        permission, persistence authority, or a View by itself.
      - PackManager operations may exist before any AppInstance exists.
      - PackManager operations may be requested from an AppInstance only
        through permitted mediated surfaces.
      - ListPacks is the general pack-listing primitive.
      - AppPack, SavePack, ViewPack, ContentPack, and ModPack listings are
        filters over Pack kind.
      - Catalog operations do not lazily rescan the filesystem unless explicit
        rescan is requested and accepted.
      - Catalog presentation preserves distinctions between existence,
        visibility, resolution eligibility, dependency satisfaction, selected
        graph membership, activation state, and capability availability.
      - PackManager scopes are explicit.
      - PackManager consumes DA-09 roots/discovery, DA-10 identity/visibility,
        DA-11 resolution, DA-13 dependency/advisory metadata, DA-14 activation,
        DA-16 capability boundaries, DA-17 policy, DA-20 AppInstance, DA-22 UI,
        DA-23/DA-27 adapter, and DA-28 RuntimeHost semantics.
      - PackManager does not silently bypass any of those semantics.
      - Advisory pack relations remain advisory unless explicit policy or user
        decision makes them part of selected graph handling.
      - Hard dependency failure remains hard failure unless an accepted policy
        or user decision explicitly resolves it under the owning documents.
      - Risk acceptance does not make a risky graph safe.
      - Expected-break evidence does not convert failure into success.
      - PackManager may prepare selected pack graphs and activation plans.
      - PackManager graph preparation does not activate, import, execute, or
        grant PackContext authority.
      - PackManager operations that perform powerful or persistent behavior
        require appropriate permission, policy, persistence, and trace handling.
      - PackManager Views are presentation and interaction surfaces, not raw
        management authority.
      - DA-29 owns PackManager-domain trace reason identifiers.
      - DA-03 owns the trace substrate, not PackManager semantics.

25.2. OPEN:
      - concrete operation identifiers, scope vocabulary, ListPacks envelopes,
        catalog fields, selected graph schema, activation-plan schema,
        capability identifiers, command syntax, RPC fields, View embedding,
        package source/search/install/update/remove protocols, SavePack export,
        active graph-change policy, and PackManager trace field schema.
